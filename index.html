I am a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Verona and UNC Chapel Hill, working on analog abstraction, mixed-signal simulation, and cyber-physical systems (CPSs) with a focus on fault simulation and functional safety in Industry 4.0.

Academic Profile

My research blends formal methods and simulation tools to evaluate reliability and optimize performance in CPSs. I'm currently a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Fellow under the STRATEGUS project.

Research Highlights

  • Analog Abstraction for fast C/C++ simulation of analog circuits (DATE 2016, TCAD 2018)
  • Fault Simulation for analog safety analysis (FDL, ETS, Springer Book Chapter)
  • Network Synthesis for embedded systems (TCOMP 2018)
  • Continuous-Time Scheduling in Industry 4.0 (IEEE CASE, ETFA 2022)
  • Multidomain Simulation Platforms (TETC, DATE)
  • EDACurry: transistor-level parser for SPICE/Spectre/Eldo (FDL 2021)
  • Transistor-Level Defect Modeling aligned with IEEE P2427
